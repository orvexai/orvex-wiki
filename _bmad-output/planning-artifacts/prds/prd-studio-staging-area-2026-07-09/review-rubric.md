# PRD Quality Review — Agent Staging Area

## Overall verdict

This is a strong, build-ready PRD carried by a genuine thesis — invert the industry's "govern-the-direct-write" bet and make the review boundary *plus* an AI-reviewer-that-merges the product — and that thesis is earned by Discovery (the 14-platform synthesis and engine-scale audit), not template furniture. Decisions are made with honest trade-offs (the addendum's rejected-alternatives section is exemplary), scope is fenced with disciplined `[ASSUMPTION]` tagging and a clean index roundtrip, counter-metrics guard the primary SMs, and the FR/UJ/SM/NFR ID spine is downstream-ready. What's at risk sits precisely on the differentiated heart: the Librarian's routing (FR-STG8), beautification (FR-STG14/21) and learning (FR-STG16) rest on adjectives — "beautiful," "measurably," "per house conventions" — and on aggregate metrics rather than per-FR testable conditions, so story creation will be softest exactly where the product's novelty concentrates. A few contract-vocabulary snags (an orphaned `divert-to-memory` Disposition; queue-category vs Disposition-enum drift) are cheap and worth fixing before this feeds architecture.

## Decision-readiness — strong

A decision-maker can act on this. The Vision (§1) states a bet in the imperative ("The Staging Area inverts that bet. Agents get one safe verb — *propose*"), the Autonomy Dial default is fixed at `recommend` (§3, FR-STG10), and Constraints (§10) make hard calls with compressed evidence inline ("Postgres-only (D-S12) — no Dolt (evidence: MySQL-wire, auto-commit batch failures…)"). The trade-offs are surfaced honestly rather than smoothed to neutral: `addendum.md` A1 rejects Dolt, staging-inside-the-wiki, govern-the-direct-write, LLM-as-approver, and cross-store ACID, each with what was given up and what was kept ("What we keep: cell-style conflict keying"); A2's options table carries an explicit "Cost:" column. Open Questions (§11) are genuinely open — rollback fidelity for deletes (OQ3), per-space vs per-doc-type dial granularity (OQ4), Card Contract v1 field-authority transfer (OQ5) are real unresolved forks, not rhetorical. The single `[NOTE FOR PM]` (§6.2) lands on the one real tension — deferring `full-auto` that Daniel asked for — and names it "emotionally load-bearing." The biggest decision (supersede/absorb vs new peer service, per `.memlog.md`) is resolved to "new service, supersede via ADR" (§9) with the residual verbatim-vs-v2 question flagged.

### Findings
- **low** Load-bearing trade-offs live in the addendum (§10 vs addendum A1–A2) — prd.md compresses the rejections to parentheticals ("no Dolt (evidence: …)"); the defensible argument (beads' production "database is read only" failure, single-writer default) is only in `addendum.md`. *Fix:* none required if the addendum ships as part of the packet — but a one-line pointer from §10 to addendum A1 would keep a prd.md-only reader from mistaking the compressed evidence for the whole case.

## Substance over theater — strong

Content is earned, not furniture. The differentiation claim is the opposite of innovation theater: "no profiled competitor ships an AI reviewer that merges — and only one stages AI writes at all" (§1) is backed by the 14-platform Discovery synthesis, and the PRD sells the boundary using competitors' documented pain (Rovo hallucinated write-success, Notion "amplifies messy workspaces," §1 / addendum A1). Personas (§2.1) are exactly four and each drives real requirements — Agent fleets → FR-STG1, Knowledge managers → FR-STG12, Customer admins → FR-STG10, Orvex operators → NFR-STG2 — none are decoration. The Vision is un-swappable: it names Orvex's specific failure mode ("~200 API round-trips, ~200 history rows, ~700 background jobs") rather than a generic knowledge-tool aspiration. NFRs (§7) carry product-specific thresholds ("submit-to-triaged p95 ≤ 5 min," "agent credentials physically cannot write wiki canonical content"), not "must be scalable/secure." The one place the framing outruns the mechanism — "constantly learns" (§4.3) for what `[ASSUMPTION: FR-STG16]` scopes to few-shot exemplars + tier recalibration — is handled honestly by the tag; I treat its testability under Done-ness rather than as theater.

_No findings._

## Strategic coherence — strong

The PRD reads as a thesis with features hung off it, not a backlog with headings. Prioritization follows the thesis: MVP (§6.1) leads with intake + triage + review queue + apply and defers `full-auto` "needs trust data first" (§6.2) — which *is* the recommend-first thesis expressed as scope. Success Metrics validate the bet rather than measure activity: SM-1 (≥99% of agent writes through staging) tests the boundary, SM-2 (recommendation acceptance ≥90% after 4 weeks) tests the earns-trust claim, SM-3 (100 proposals cleared in ≤10 interactions) tests the scale claim. Counter-metrics are present and real — SM-C1 forces auto-apply to *follow* acceptance evidence, SM-C2 guards against volume-as-success — which is unusual discipline. The one coherence stress is that §4.4's standing maintenance mandate (dedup, staleness, structure, beautification) is a second product arc bolted onto the write-boundary thesis; the PRD keeps it coherent by routing every sweep into "the same Review Queue Marta already works" under "the same dial" (UJ-4), and the MVP wisely reduces it to recommend-only flagging (§6.2 defers "structure/beautification sweeps beyond flagging").

### Findings
- **low** Maintenance mandate is a second arc (§4.4, FR-STG18–22) — dilutes the tight "safe write boundary" thesis into "the Librarian owns all wiki quality." *Fix:* none needed for MVP (already reduced to recommend-only flagging); keep an eye that v1.1 sweep expansion doesn't outgrow the intake spine that justifies the shared queue.

## Done-ness clarity — adequate

For roughly 22 of 26 FRs an engineer would know what "done" means, and the bar is high: hard latency numbers (FR-STG1 "p95 submit latency ≤ 500ms"), an enumerated ChangeSet state machine (FR-STG2), interaction ceilings (FR-STG12 "≤ 10 human interactions"), a testable negative ("Never silent three-way prose merge," FR-STG9), and credential-layer isolation stated as a physical impossibility (NFR-STG5). The reason this is *adequate* and not *strong* is that the softness is concentrated on the differentiated features, exactly where story creation will lean hardest. FR-STG14 / FR-STG21 beautification asks content be "re-authored to… beautiful ProseMirror… per house conventions" and sweeps "identify structurally poor pages" — the mechanical half (only schema-registered nodes; verify by full-text + effective-marks equality) is testable, but "beautiful," "structurally poor," and the "house conventions" reference (not located or linked) give no acceptance criterion. FR-STG16 claims feedback "measurably adjusts future behavior" with no measurement design or attribution baseline; SM-2 is the de-facto acceptance test but the FR doesn't say so, and nothing distinguishes "the Librarian learned" from "producers improved." FR-STG8 routing correctness ("a Proposal matching an existing concept becomes an amend… never a sibling") is an AI judgment verifiable only in aggregate (SM-2/SM-5), not per-proposal — acceptable, but the PRD should say so rather than imply a per-call guarantee.

### Findings
- **medium** Beautification has no testable done-condition (FR-STG14 §4.2, FR-STG21 §4.4) — "beautiful," "structurally poor," "per house conventions" are adjectives; the house conventions aren't cited to a page/slug. Story creation can't derive acceptance criteria for the merge/beautify path. *Fix:* pin "house conventions" to a wiki canon slug and give beautification a mechanical done-bar (e.g., "renders TL;DR lead + at least one structured node where source had ≥N list items; zero unknown nodes after apply"), leaving aesthetic quality to SM/eval, not the FR.
- **medium** "Learning" lacks a measurement design (FR-STG16 §4.3; "constantly learns," §4.3 intro) — "measurably adjusts future behavior" has no baseline, no attribution, no pointer to SM-2. The moat feature is the hardest to prove done. *Fix:* wire FR-STG16 to SM-2 explicitly and name the measurement (per-tenant acceptance-rate trend over a fixed window with a control for producer Trust-Tier drift), so "learning happened" is falsifiable.
- **low** Routing correctness is aggregate-only (FR-STG8 §4.2) — per-proposal find-before-create correctness isn't testable; it rides SM-2/SM-5. *Fix:* state in FR-STG8 that routing accuracy is measured in aggregate against SM-2, not asserted per-call, so story authors don't write a per-proposal correctness test that can't exist.

## Scope honesty — strong

Omissions are explicit and de-scoping is done out loud, not silently. §5 carries six Non-Goals doing real work ("No three-way automatic prose merging, ever"; "Not a second wiki"), §2.2 fences Non-Users with a `[NON-GOAL for MVP]`, and §6.2 lists what's out of v1 *with reasons* ("`full-auto` — needs trust data first"). The `[ASSUMPTION]` discipline is the strongest mechanical feature of the PRD: I checked the roundtrip and it is clean — every inline tag (FR-STG2, STG7, STG13, STG16, STG19, STG26, §5, §9, §10, and NFR-STG3) appears in the §12 Assumptions Index, and every index entry maps back to an inline tag. Open-items density (~6 OQs + ~10 assumptions + 1 NOTE FOR PM) is proportionate for a new cross-service platform of this scope — none of the open items block architecture from starting (they are parameter values and enforcement-location questions), so this clears the green-light bar rather than tripping it.

### Findings
- **low** An assumed value is elevated to a hard contract without the caveat traveling (FR-STG7 §4.2 vs NFR-STG1 §7 / SM-3) — FR-STG7 tags the triage SLO `[ASSUMPTION: SLO value]`, but NFR-STG1 states the same "p95 ≤ 5 min" as "the benchmarked contract… Load-tested in CI" and SM-3 gates release on it. A number can't be both unsettled and a CI release gate. *Fix:* either commit the SLO (drop the assumption tag) or mark NFR-STG1/SM-3 as provisional-pending-benchmark; don't gate a release on an admitted guess.

## Downstream usability — adequate

This PRD explicitly feeds architecture/epics (§0), so this dimension carries weight — and the spine is genuinely extractable. The Glossary (§3) is present with 13 terms; FR/UJ/SM/NFR IDs are contiguous, unique, and cross-references resolve (FR-STG4 "Realizes FR-STG9"; the §8 "Validates FR-…" back-references all land; §9's external `FR-SA12–16` are clearly cross-doc). The dings are vocabulary, not structure. The Glossary lists `divert-to-memory` as a first-class Disposition value, but no "memory" target is defined anywhere in the PRD and no FR handles a divert-to-memory path — an architect building the Disposition enum as specified would implement a route with no destination. UJ-2's review-queue buckets ("auto-apply-eligible," "recommended-accept," "flagged," §2.3) don't map onto the Glossary Disposition enum (`apply-as-routed / apply-with-merge / duplicate-of / conflict / reject / divert-to-memory / escalate`), leaving UX/story work to guess the correspondence. And several FRs embed prior-art shorthand in the requirement text itself ("Stack Overflow valve; Wikipedia backlog lesson," FR-STG3; "the Google-Docs-12-year-gap capability," FR-STG12; "LangGraph checkpointer lesson," FR-STG6) — useful anchors for the architect, but noise a story author must strip to find the testable requirement.

### Findings
- **medium** Orphaned `divert-to-memory` Disposition (§3 Glossary) — appears in the Disposition enum with no referent: no memory target defined, not in MVP scope (§6.1), no FR. Downstream will build an enum value with no behavior. *Fix:* define the memory-divert target and its FR, or drop the value from the v1 enum and note it as a post-v1 Disposition (the cross-AI-memory thread is de-scoped per `.memlog.md`, so dropping it is likely correct).
- **low** Queue categories don't map to the Disposition enum (UJ-2 §2.3 vs §3) — "auto-apply-eligible / recommended-accept / flagged" is a coarser UI layer than the seven-value enum; the correspondence is unstated. *Fix:* add a one-line mapping (which Dispositions roll up into which queue bucket) to §3 or FR-STG12.
- **low** Prior-art shorthand embedded in FRs (FR-STG3, STG6, STG11, STG12 §4) — parenthetical exemplars muddy the testable requirement for source-extraction. *Fix:* move the "(X pattern/lesson)" anchors to a rationale note or the addendum; keep the FR body to the requirement + its consequence.

## Shape fit — strong

The rigor matches the product. This is a cross-service platform capability with a real human review surface, and the PRD is correctly FR-heavy (26 FRs, contracts, NFRs) *and* carries UJs where they are load-bearing — the Review Queue is a genuine human experience for knowledge managers and admins, so UJ-1/2/3 with named human protagonists (Sage, Marta, Priya) earn their place rather than being over-formalization. The brownfield facet is handled honestly: §9 plus addendum A3 carry accurate existing-code references (the 501 wiki-api facade, `save_page`/`edit` writing docmost directly via DocmostWrapper, the 11+8 MCP tool inventory) and distinguish what's superseded from what's new. The only shape wrinkle is UJ-4 ("The Librarian keeps the shelves tidy"), whose protagonist is the Librarian itself — a system-actor narrative of §4.4 dressed as a user journey, with no human until "the same review queue Marta already works."

### Findings
- **low** UJ-4 has no human protagonist (§2.3) — it's a system-behavior narrative, not a user journey; mild over-formalization. *Fix:* give it Marta reviewing the morning sweep output (which is what actually happens), or reclassify it as a feature narrative under §4.4.
