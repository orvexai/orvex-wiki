# PRD: Agent Staging Area — staged agent writes + the Librarian

**In short:** Agents never write a customer's wiki directly. Every agent-proposed change — add a document, add a section, edit, replace, delete — lands as a Proposal in a dedicated staging store *outside* the wiki, grouped per session into a ChangeSet. The **Librarian**, an AI reviewer with a per-customer-tweakable prompt and an autonomy dial that defaults to *recommend, not act*, reviews, routes, merges and beautifies staged content into the wiki, learns continuously from human feedback on its recommendations, and carries a standing mandate to keep the whole wiki tidy. Built as a new cross-service platform capability (new service; `orvex-studio-api`'s Curator becomes a client), exposed as new **staging** sections of the Studio MCP server and the platform CLI.

## 1. Vision

Today an Orvex customer's agents either write the wiki directly (the current MCP `save_page`/`edit` tools) or don't capture knowledge at all. Direct writes at fleet scale wreck the wiki: the engine writes a full-content history row per save into an unbounded table, offers no cross-page batch primitive, no throttle on updates, and no review boundary — a single enthusiastic chat session updating 100 documents produces ~200 API round-trips, ~200 history rows, ~700 background jobs and an unreviewable mess. The market's answer (Atlassian Rovo, Notion Agents, SharePoint) is to let agents write directly and govern with permissions and audit — and their communities already report hallucinated write-success and "AI amplifying messy workspaces."

The Staging Area inverts that bet. Agents get one safe verb — *propose* — and a place where proposals are cheap, reviewable and disposable. The Librarian turns raw proposals into a curated wiki: it recommends dispositions per ChangeSet, routes content to the right living page (find-before-create, never a duplicate sibling), merges at section level, and re-authors content into beautiful ProseMirror-native pages. Humans stay the approval authority by default; customers who want speed turn the autonomy dial up, and the Librarian earns that trust by learning from every accept, reject and edit. Because no profiled competitor ships an AI reviewer that merges — and only one stages AI writes at all — this is the differentiated heart of Orvex Studio's knowledge story.

## 2. Target User

### 2.1 Jobs To Be Done
- **Agent fleets** (ChatGPT-portal assistants, Claude agents, CLI agents): "when I finish a conversation with something worth saving, I need a one-call way to save it that can't damage the wiki."
- **Knowledge managers / curators** (customer humans): "let me review a morning's worth of agent proposals in minutes, not per-page archaeology; show me diffs, not dumps."
- **Customer admins:** "let me tune how aggressive the Librarian is — my regulated team wants human sign-off on everything; my startup team wants full auto."
- **Orvex operators:** "agent write bursts must never degrade the wiki product or other tenants."

### 2.2 Non-Users (v1)
- End readers of the wiki (they see only applied results).
- Human wiki editors using the Docmost editor — their direct edit path is untouched.
- Third-party platforms wanting a bespoke integration portal — the public surfaces are MCP, the CLI, and the service's pinned OpenAPI contract (family seam), nothing bespoke per platform.

### 2.3 Key User Journeys

- **UJ-1. Sage lands a conversation's knowledge without touching the wiki.**
  Sage, a support agent on a customer's ChatGPT portal, finishes a session that surfaced a new rate-limit workaround. Following its prompt pack ("when you finish a conversation and you've got something worth saving, save it to the staging area"), it calls `staging_propose` once with a draft section and its evidence. The proposal joins the session's open ChangeSet; Sage gets back a receipt with the proposal ID and disposition forecast ("likely: amend `runbook-rate-limits`"). The wiki is byte-for-byte unchanged. **Edge case:** the staging quota for the session is exhausted → Sage receives a hard admission-control rejection with the queue-full reason, tells the user, and does not retry-spam.

- **UJ-2. Marta clears 100 proposals over coffee.**
  Marta, knowledge manager at a 40-agent customer whose spaces run the default `recommend` dial, opens the review queue at 9am: one ChangeSet, "Session #4821 — 100 proposals", already triaged and beautified by the Librarian into *62 auto-apply-eligible recommendations* (append/add-section from high-trust producers — the set that would have auto-applied at a higher dial), *31 recommended-accept with per-item diff + target page*, and *7 flagged* (2 conflicts with newer wiki edits, 3 low-confidence routes each posed as a one-question ask, 2 destructive replace/delete). She bulk-accepts the 62 after skimming a sample, works the 31 with filtered bulk actions, answers the 7 flags one-by-one, and hits Apply. The apply engine executes the accepted set against the wiki with live progress and per-item receipts (a mid-apply pause would leave an explicit partial state with a resume/abort decision — never silent). **Climax:** ~100 documents updated with about 15 minutes of human attention. **Edge case:** two proposals target a page a human edited an hour ago → hard conflict, never silent merge; Marta picks rebase-and-re-review or reject.

- **UJ-3. Priya turns the dial up — and the Librarian earns it.**
  Priya, admin at a fast-moving startup tenant, sets the autonomy dial from `recommend` to `auto-apply-low-risk`. For two weeks the Librarian's acceptance rate on recommendations has been 96%; its weekly digest shows what it auto-applied and why. When it misroutes a page, Priya rejects with a one-line reason; the feedback becomes a labeled example that adjusts future routing recommendations. Later she flips the marketing space to `full-auto` while keeping the compliance space at `human-review-everything`. **Resolution:** autonomy is earned, scoped, and reversible.

- **UJ-4. Marta reviews the overnight tidy sweep.**
  Overnight, the Librarian's maintenance sweep flags: 3 near-duplicate pages (recommends supersede-merge), 14 pages past their verification window (queues re-review), 2 orphaned pages and 6 broken links (recommends fixes), and 4 pages failing the beautification bar (proposes diffs). Marta finds it all in the same review queue she already works each morning, batch-accepts the link fixes, takes the duplicate merges one by one, and leaves the beautification diffs for Friday. Nothing changed in the wiki until she accepted — because this space's dial says `recommend`.

## 3. Glossary

- **Proposal** — the atomic staged unit: one intended change to one wiki document, carrying an Operation, target reference, content payload (DfM or ProseMirror JSON), base-version stamp, provenance, and Librarian annotations. Descendant of Card Contract v1's "card".
- **Operation** — one of `add-document`, `add-section`, `edit-section`, `replace-document`, `delete-document` (the five ops Daniel specified). Sub-document ops are keyed by `(document_id, section_anchor)` — Dolt-cell-style conflict keying — where `section_anchor` resolves against **stable block IDs** (the engine's UniqueID extension), never heading text; document-level ops (`add-document`, `replace-document`, `delete-document`) are whole-doc-keyed.
- **ChangeSet** — the named, first-class container grouping a session's Proposals; the unit of review, scheduling, apply and rollback (Sanity Release / Veeva DCC pattern). One producing session ⇒ one open ChangeSet by default.
- **Staging Store** — the dedicated Postgres store (own service, own schema) holding Proposals, ChangeSets, decisions, feedback and the maintenance queue. Explicitly **not** the wiki database (Daniel: "would prefer not").
- **Librarian** — the AI reviewer/curator component: triages Proposals, recommends dispositions, routes to living pages, merges, beautifies, and runs maintenance sweeps. Per-tenant prompt + policy.
- **Librarian Prompt Pack** — the per-customer-tweakable prompt/policy bundle governing the Librarian's judgment, plus the agent-facing instructions ("save to staging when…") installed into customer agent platforms.
- **Autonomy Dial** — per-tenant, per-space setting: `recommend` (default) → `auto-apply-low-risk` → `full-auto`. Destructive Operations (`replace-document`, `delete-document`) always require human review below `full-auto`.
- **Disposition** — the Librarian's recommended outcome for a Proposal: `apply-as-routed` / `apply-with-merge` / `duplicate-of` / `conflict` / `reject` / `divert-to-workgraph` (formerly `divert-to-memory`) / `escalate`.
- **Trust Tier** — per-producer (agent identity) standing derived from historical acceptance; gates auto-apply eligibility (Wikipedia autopromotion pattern).
- **Review Queue** — the human surface listing ChangeSets and maintenance recommendations with filtered bulk accept/reject/merge.
- **Apply** — the Librarian executing accepted Proposals against the wiki via the sanctioned write path (ProseMirror JSON / DfM, CAS base-version, draft→ratify contracts where applicable).
- **Feedback Event** — a human decision on a recommendation (accept / reject / edit + optional reason); the Librarian's learning signal.
- **Maintenance Sweep** — the Librarian's standing tidy pass: duplicate detection, staleness/verification queue, structure & link hygiene, beautification proposals.

## 4. Features

### 4.1 Staged Proposal Intake
**Description:** The only agent write path into customer knowledge. Agents submit Proposals via new **staging** sections of the Studio MCP server and platform CLI; submission is cheap, append-only, and never blocks on review (OPS Intake's "nothing in the wiki yet, never interrupts"). Realizes UJ-1.

- **FR-STG1: Submit Proposal.** An authenticated agent can submit a Proposal with Operation, target (existing page ref or placement hint), content (DfM or ProseMirror JSON — lossy formats rejected, mirroring the engine contract), rationale, and source-conversation reference. Consequences: submission is a single MCP/CLI call; the wiki DB is untouched; receipt includes proposal ID + ChangeSet ID; p95 submit latency ≤ 500ms at 100-proposal bursts.
- **FR-STG2: ChangeSet lifecycle.** Proposals group into a per-session ChangeSet (`open → submitted → in-review → partially-accepted/accepted → applying → applied / rolled-back / rejected / expired`). An agent can submit the ChangeSet explicitly ("landing the plane") or it auto-submits on session end/timeout. `[ASSUMPTION: default auto-submit after 30 min idle]`
- **FR-STG3: Admission control & quotas.** Per-session and per-tenant in-flight caps with reject-at-the-door semantics and explicit error codes; queue depth/age alerting from day one (Stack Overflow valve; Wikipedia backlog lesson). Backpressure events are visible to tenant admins. Caps are plan entitlements (billing is SoR).
- **FR-STG4: Base-version stamping.** Every Proposal targeting an existing page records the page's current version/updated-at at submit time for later conflict detection. Realizes FR-STG9.
- **FR-STG5: Provenance.** Two-sided immutable attribution on every Proposal: producing agent identity AND triggering end-user conversation/session (Notion pattern); provenance survives into applied wiki history and the audit trail.
- **FR-STG6: Pointer-state payloads.** Large content bodies are stored by reference (object storage) with pointers in queue rows, so 100 pending Proposals cost storage only, no pinned compute (LangGraph checkpointer lesson).

### 4.2 The Librarian — review, routing & merge
**Description:** The Librarian triages every ChangeSet: routes each Proposal to its living page (find-before-create), classifies, detects duplicates and conflicts, and produces per-item Dispositions with diffs. It **recommends by default; it acts only as far as the Autonomy Dial allows** (Daniel). Realizes UJ-2, UJ-3.

- **FR-STG7: Disposition per Proposal.** For every Proposal the Librarian produces a Disposition + target route + **routing-confidence score** (a property of this route decision — distinct from the producer's Trust Tier) + rendered diff + one-line justification, within SLO (p95 ≤ 5 min per 100-Proposal ChangeSet) `[ASSUMPTION: SLO value — provisional until the NFR-STG1 benchmark]`. LLM calls delegate to `orvex-studio-ai`; the staging service owns orchestration, not model access.
- **FR-STG8: Find-before-create routing.** Routing resolves against the wiki's living tree + the knowledge service's search/dedup surface; a Proposal matching an existing concept becomes an amend of that node rather than a sibling page — an accuracy **target** measured as the duplicate-sibling rate (SM-5), not an absolute over a probabilistic matcher. Duplicate candidates surface as `duplicate-of` Dispositions. Content judged working-context rather than durable wiki knowledge receives the `divert-to-workgraph` Disposition: on accept it is packaged and submitted to the Workgraph service — `orvex-studio-workgraph`, the renamed cross-agent coordination service (PO 2026-07-10; "memory" now names only the user-managed memory product) — as an item/note (sibling PRD FR-MEM1) with two-way cross-links — the Wiki · Memory · Trash shelf routing from the OPS design, realized across the two services (`reject` covers Trash). Routing accuracy is verified in aggregate via SM-2 acceptance and SM-5 duplicate-rate trends, not asserted per-proposal.
- **FR-STG9: Hard conflict detection.** Before apply, each Proposal re-checks its base-version against the live page **through the authoritative content path (wiki-api/ydoc-backed read), not the engine's debounce-lagged content column (≤ 45s staleness)**; triage diffs are stamped with their source version and re-rendered before apply if the page version moved. A moved base at the Proposal's conflict key yields a `conflict` Disposition requiring human resolution (rebase-and-re-review or reject). When multiple section Proposals target one page, the apply engine sequences them and re-resolves each subsequent anchor against stable block IDs after every write; an anchor lost to the preceding write (or to server-side node-stripping/coalescing) is a `conflict`, never a guess. **Never silent three-way prose merge** (no-fallbacks rule; nobody in the field auto-merges prose).
- **FR-STG10: Autonomy Dial enforcement.** Per-tenant + per-space dial: `recommend` (default — every apply needs a human accept), `auto-apply-low-risk` (only `add-section`/`edit-section`/`add-document` from producers above a Trust-Tier threshold; destructive ops always human-gated), `full-auto` (Librarian applies everything, humans audit post-hoc). **All three positions ship in v1** (PO decision 2026-07-10 — nothing deferred); the default remains `recommend`, and SM-C1 still requires auto-apply growth to *follow* acceptance evidence per tenant, not precede it. Every auto-apply records a justification line (SharePoint delegated-change pattern). The dial is server-enforced policy, not prompt text.
- **FR-STG11: Trust Tiers.** Per-producer acceptance history computes a Trust Tier that gates auto-apply eligibility and throttles repeat-rejected producers (SO submitter gate). Tiers are visible to admins and recomputed continuously.
- **FR-STG12: Batch review UX.** The Review Queue offers filtered bulk operations (accept/reject by Disposition class, by producer, by target space, by risk) with per-item drill-down diffs — the Google-Docs-12-year-gap capability, built as a core primitive. 100-Proposal ChangeSets must be clearable with ≤ 10 human interactions in the common case. Queue buckets roll up Dispositions deterministically: *auto-apply-eligible* = `apply-as-routed`/`apply-with-merge` on low-risk Operations from producers above the Trust-Tier threshold; *recommended-accept* = the same Dispositions otherwise; *flagged* = `duplicate-of`, `conflict`, `escalate`, `divert-to-workgraph`, and all destructive Operations; *recommended-reject* = `reject`. Routes below the routing-confidence threshold (FR-STG7) always join *flagged* as a **mandatory per-item adjudication lane** — presented doc-amend-style as one plain-English question each (candidate page title + one-line diff of what would change) — and are excluded from bulk-accept regardless of Disposition, so the characteristic Librarian failure (a confident mis-route) cannot hide inside a 2-second bulk skim.
- **FR-STG13: Apply engine.** Accepted Proposals apply via the sanctioned wiki write path: ProseMirror JSON/DfM with CAS (`--if-version` semantics), per-page block ops where finer-grained, sequenced with per-item receipts; ChangeSet-level all-or-nothing is a workflow-state gate (Veeva MDCC), not a cross-store transaction — the ChangeSet cannot flip to `applied` while any member is unresolved, and partial failures pause with explicit state, never silent partial success. **Stated plainly: a paused mid-apply ChangeSet means a partially-mutated live wiki** — the pause state names applied vs pending members and forces an explicit human resume/abort decision. Rollback: `replace-document`/`delete-document` take a **snapshot-before-mutate** into the Staging Store (full-fidelity restore); additive/edit Operations roll back via compensating inverse Proposals — closing the delete-rollback fidelity question.
- **FR-STG14: Beautification at triage (review-what-you-apply).** The Librarian re-authors Proposal content to the wiki's full ProseMirror capability (callouts, tables, columns, diagrams, TL;DR lead) **during triage, before review — the rendered diff the reviewer approves IS the final content**; apply (FR-STG13) then performs mechanical engine writes only, keeping beautify LLM latency out of the apply SLO. Re-authoring scope matches the Operation's granularity: section-scoped Operations beautify ONLY the affected section; whole-page re-authoring happens only for `add-document`/`replace-document` (which are whole-doc-keyed anyway) — never a whole-page rewrite riding a section edit (that would collapse section-level conflict keying, FR-STG9). Per the family authoring conventions `[ASSUMPTION: conventions canon slug in orvexstudioarch pinned at wiki handoff]`. Mechanical done-bar per beautified unit: (a) TL;DR/lead callout present (document-level ops); (b) only schema-registered nodes — zero stripped-unknown-node warnings; (c) contiguous heading hierarchy; (d) enumerable source content (≥ 5 list-ish items, tabular data) renders as a structured node, not a prose wall; (e) apply verified by full-text + effective-marks equality per affected section (the engine coalesces adjacent same-mark runs). Aesthetic quality beyond the bar is measured via SM-2 acceptance and periodic human delight-review, not per-FR assertion.

### 4.3 Librarian learning loop
**Description:** The Librarian "constantly learns based on feedback" (Daniel). Every human decision is a labeled example that tunes future recommendations.

- **FR-STG15: Feedback capture.** Every accept/reject/edit on a recommendation is recorded as a Feedback Event with optional reason, linked to the Proposal, Disposition and prompt-pack version that produced it.
- **FR-STG16: Learning application.** Feedback measurably adjusts future behavior per tenant: routing/classification exemplars are drawn from recent Feedback Events (few-shot injection), Trust Tiers update, and the Librarian periodically proposes revisions to its own tenant prompt pack — which are themselves recommendations for the admin to accept (never self-applied). `[ASSUMPTION: v1 learning = feedback-informed exemplars + tier recalibration + proposed prompt revisions; no fine-tuning]` Learning effect is validated by a held-out experiment, not the raw SM-2 trend (which is confounded by producer improvement and reviewer drift): exemplars-on vs. exemplars-off routing/classification accuracy compared on a rolling labeled set drawn from Feedback Events, with Trust-Tier composition held constant; every exemplar injection and tier recalibration is logged for attribution (FR-STG17).
- **FR-STG17: Learning transparency.** Admins see recommendation acceptance rate over time, per-category error breakdown, and a changelog of prompt-pack revisions with their measured effect — covering both manual pack edits and Librarian-proposed self-revisions (both v1; a proposed revision that is never accepted still appears with its rejection).
- **FR-STG27: Cold-start defaults.** New tenants ship with a baseline Prompt Pack, a conservative default posture (dial = `recommend`; empty Trust Tiers ⇒ nothing auto-apply-eligible), and generic starter exemplars that embed no other tenant's content — the day-1 experience is recommendation-only, stated as such in onboarding. Any future cross-tenant priors must be content-free by construction.

### 4.4 Librarian maintenance — the standing tidy mandate
**Description:** Beyond intake, the Librarian "will maintain the wiki as well, keep it tidy" (Daniel). Sweeps generate recommendations into the same Review Queue, governed by the same dial. Default sweep scope: write-proposals target agent-authored pages; human-authored pages receive flag-only findings unless the tenant opts in — the human editing path stays untouched (§2.2). Maintenance recommendations carry their own daily interaction budget, separate from intake ChangeSets (NFR-STG1). Realizes UJ-4.

- **FR-STG18: Duplicate & merge sweep.** Corpus-scale near-duplicate detection with supersede/merge recommendations (whole-doc supersession to archive with two-sided links — P5 semantics).
- **FR-STG19: Staleness & verification queue.** Guru-style freshness: verifier + next-review-due stamps on applied pages, re-verification queue when due or when drift signals fire, and retrieval trust gating (agents ground only on content above a trust threshold). Enforcement lives inside `orvex-studio-knowledge` and is a **v1 dependency deliverable** (PO 2026-07-10 — not contract-only); the gating contract itself lands in `orvex-studio-contracts`.
- **FR-STG20: Structure & link hygiene.** Orphans, broken links, misfiled pages, IA-conformance flags with recommended fixes.
- **FR-STG21: Beautification sweep.** Identifies pages failing the FR-STG14 mechanical bar (plus broken embeds/render errors) and proposes beautification diffs (never auto below the dial threshold).
- **FR-STG22: AI-groundability flag + review-state gate.** Pages can be marked human-visible but not AI-groundable/editable, decoupled from human ACLs (SharePoint pattern); the Librarian and agent retrieval respect it. Additionally, content applied **without** human review (auto-applied under the dial) is not agent-groundable until a human has reviewed it — post-hoc review flips the flag. This breaks the AI-writes→AI-grounds→AI-writes amplification loop the Vision indicts in competitors; SM-C3 guards it.

### 4.5 Platform surfaces & migration
**Description:** Staging is a platform capability with first-class MCP + CLI sections; agent direct-write to the wiki is removed, not deprecated-in-place (no-fallbacks rule).

- **FR-STG23: MCP staging section.** New tools on the Studio MCP server: `staging_propose`, `staging_changeset_submit`, `staging_changeset_status`, `staging_list_mine`, `staging_withdraw`, plus reviewer-scoped `staging_review_queue`, `staging_decide` (bulk-capable), `staging_apply`. Bidirectional surface: the same server grounds reads and accepts proposals (Stack Internal pattern). Machine-to-machine auth with per-agent credentials — never a shared service credential.
- **FR-STG24: CLI staging section.** `orvex-cli staging` namespace with parity verbs (`propose`, `changeset submit/status/list`, `review`, `decide`, `apply`) for CI and human power users.
- **FR-STG25: Hard-cut migration of ALL agent wiki-write surfaces.** The cut enumerates every agent write path, not just the MCP heroes: (a) MCP `save_page`/`edit` re-point to emit Proposals; (b) the hidden `studio_library_save`/`studio_librarian_session` passthroughs are absorbed or removed; (c) **the CLI path** — `docmost-cli`/`orvex-cli wiki` write verbs (`page create/update/patch/edit/upsert`, `page block`) under agent-class credentials are rejected at the wiki-api ingress (403 + migration error naming `orvex-cli staging`), which also converts today's doc-librarian/doc-amend skill chain into a staging client. Direct wiki writes remain only for human credentials and the Librarian's apply engine (credential-level enforcement per NFR-STG5 — not session-type heuristics). Failures are loud migration errors — no silent dual-write, no legacy shim. **Sequencing invariant: the hard cut is the LAST release step, gated on staging triaging→applying end-to-end in production against non-501 `orvex-studio-ai` and `orvex-studio-knowledge`; until that gate passes, agents keep the current path** (a dead knowledge-write capability with no fallback would otherwise be guaranteed by the no-fallbacks rule itself).
- **FR-STG26: Events on the spine.** Every staging state change emits a typed CloudEvent (`studio.staging.*` subdomain — requires contracts-catalog addition) `[ASSUMPTION: new subdomain approved via ADR]`; staging events carry queue, decision and audit telemetry — console consumes queue metrics. **Wiki reindexing rides the ENGINE's `wiki.*` events emitted by the applied writes (the single mutation source of truth) — staging events never trigger a second reindex** (refined 2026-07-10 to match architecture AD-9; the engine outbox becoming a live `wiki.*` producer is thereby a gating prerequisite alongside FR-STG25's non-501 dependencies).

### 4.6 Scheduling & ecosystem
**Description:** Pulled into v1 by PO decision (2026-07-10 — nothing deferred).

- **FR-STG28: Scheduled ChangeSet publishing.** An accepted ChangeSet (or accepted subset) can be scheduled to apply at a future time (the Sanity Content Release scheduling pattern): timezone-aware, cancellable until apply-start; conflict detection (FR-STG9) re-runs at apply time, so a scheduled apply against moved pages pauses exactly like an immediate one.
- **FR-STG29: Librarian prompt-pack marketplace (v1-minimal).** Tenants can publish and install Librarian Prompt Pack templates through the Studio marketplace surface `[ASSUMPTION: the existing studio marketplace seam (studio_marketplace_* tools) hosts pack listings]`. Packs are versioned and content-free of tenant data by construction (FR-STG27's rule); an install lands as a proposed pack revision routed through the admin-approval path (FR-STG16) — never silently active. v1-minimal = publish / install / rate; no revenue share. Included by PO decision over the usual marketplace-maturity caution — flagged as a scope risk, not re-litigated.

## 5. Non-Goals (Explicit)
- Not a general human collaboration/review tool for hand-authored wiki edits — humans keep editing the wiki directly.
- Not a second wiki: the staging store holds proposals and queue state, never long-lived canonical content.
- Not an autonomous editor-in-chief by default: full-auto exists, but the product's default posture is recommend-first (Daniel).
- No three-way automatic prose merging, ever.
- Public surfaces are MCP + CLI + the pinned OpenAPI contract; no bespoke third-party portal or webhook fan-out product.
- Does not replace the doc-ratify human gate for canonical status — the Librarian applies content; page status promotion keeps its existing human-ratification contract. `[ASSUMPTION: applied agent content lands at wiki status draft/published per tenant policy, canonical stays human-ratified]`

## 7. Cross-Cutting NFRs
- **NFR-STG1 Scale:** 100-Proposal single-session ChangeSet is the benchmarked contract: submit-to-triaged p95 ≤ 5 min (including per-Proposal LLM classification and beautification — see the concurrency note), human bulk-clear ≤ 10 interactions, apply ≤ 15 min (engine writes only; beautification happens at triage, FR-STG14) with zero partial-silent failures. The numeric targets are provisional initial values — the v1 load benchmark fixes them, and **the benchmark cannot bind until `orvex-studio-ai` (classify) and `orvex-studio-knowledge` (search/dedup) are past their current 501 scaffolds**; the benchmark run against real dependencies is the release gate, and its measured envelope becomes the published contract. The SLO assumes stated concurrency (parallel per-Proposal triage) within a per-tenant `orvex-studio-ai` rate budget named in the contracts seam. The intake-ChangeSet interaction budget is separate from the maintenance-sweep queue (which carries its own daily budget) — the two share a surface, not a budget. No vendor in the field publishes this; we do.
- **NFR-STG2 Isolation:** staging never degrades the wiki: apply-rate throttling toward the engine, per-tenant fairness, and zero engine schema coupling (own Postgres, contracts-only integration).
- **NFR-STG3 Durability:** Proposals survive service restarts; ChangeSet apply is resumable (checkpointed workflow via the platform workflow tier) `[ASSUMPTION: Temporal-backed via orvex-studio-workflows]`.
- **NFR-STG4 Auditability:** every recommendation, decision, auto-apply justification and apply receipt is an audit event on the spine; decision history is immutable.

## 8. Success Metrics
**Primary**
- **SM-1:** 100% of agent knowledge writes flow through staging post-cut — the hard cut plus the wiki-api 403 ingress make direct agent wiki writes structurally impossible; any non-zero direct-write count is a leak alarm (it means the cut has a hole), not an accepted tolerance. Validates FR-STG25, NFR-STG5.
- **SM-2:** Librarian recommendation acceptance rate ≥ 90% per tenant after 4 weeks of feedback learning. Validates FR-STG7/15/16.
- **SM-3:** 100-Proposal ChangeSet cleared with ≤ 10 human interactions and applied within the benchmark-fixed SLO (initial targets per NFR-STG1, finalized by the v1 load benchmark). Validates FR-STG12/13, NFR-STG1.

**Secondary**
- **SM-4:** median review-queue age < 24h across tenants (no Wikipedia-style rot). Validates FR-STG3.
- **SM-5:** duplicate-page rate in tenant wikis declines quarter-over-quarter after maintenance sweeps ship. Validates FR-STG18.

**Counter-metrics (do not optimize)**
- **SM-C1:** auto-apply share of applies — rising auto-apply must follow acceptance-rate evidence, never precede it. Counterbalances SM-2/SM-3.
- **SM-C2:** proposal volume — more staged writes is not success if acceptance falls; watch acceptance-weighted volume. Counterbalances SM-1.
- **SM-C3:** share of agent grounding citations sourced from auto-applied-but-unreviewed content — zero by construction (FR-STG22's review-state gate); any non-zero value is a leak alarm. Counterbalances SM-C1's pressure to raise the dial.
