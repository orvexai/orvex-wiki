## 🗺️ Epic — Foundation, extraction & platform seams

orvex-studio-api is the Studio product BFF, **physically extracted** from `orvex-prompt-studio-poc/apps/bff` (Orvex-owned, not AGPL — free reuse, module eviction, not a clean-room rebuild — A-STRANGLE). Before any product surface can ship, the extracted service needs the platform spine every other epic stands on: a real layered TS/Hono codebase with CI, edge auth resolving the **polymorphic {user|org} principal** (D-S17), fail-closed **cell isolation** (A-CELL / A-CELL-COMPLIANCE), the Kafka-first **transactional-outbox** producer + idempotent consumer path, the Postgres/Redis/S3 store topology (A-DATA), and house observability. Nothing here is product-visible on its own; everything above it is.

This epic exists because the audit found an *honest scaffold* — a 59-line `index.ts`, no layering, no tests, no CI, no lockfile — and named the two HIGH structural tensions (Go↔TS auth verify; producer dual-write, now fixed to an own-outbox design). It turns that scaffold into a service.

**Why (cites):** A-STRANGLE, A-AUTH, A-CELL, A-CELL-COMPLIANCE, A-DATA (arch `ekTh7nDQqo`); FR-SA29/31/33/34, NFR-SA6/7/8 (PRD `85qj2wwU2L`); Brief delivery doctrine §2 "build the shared chokepoints before anything else."
