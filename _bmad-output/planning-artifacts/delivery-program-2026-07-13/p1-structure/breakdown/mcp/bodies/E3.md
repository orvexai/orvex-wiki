## 🗺️ Feature area

This is the heart of the post-split repoint: the MCP moves from "one Docmost engine upstream + a placeholder Studio issuer + codegen from a live 322-path AGPL descriptor" to **three named upstreams** (wiki-api, ai, knowledge) fed by a **contracts-pinned, fail-loud typed client**. The wiki verbs re-aim onto wiki-api's byte-compatible facade first (base-URL only, R0), then onto the `/v1` verb grammar via **one deliberate regeneration** at the contracts freeze (R2). `ask` cuts over to **orvex-studio-ai** (D-M7, ADR-0001), and search/related/neighborhood reach **orvex-studio-knowledge directly** (D-M8, PO ruling 9). Codegen sources the pinned `orvex-studio-contracts` tag — never a live descriptor (canon P3 forbids AGPL-derived codegen).

It exists because "build in isolation against pinned contracts" is impossible while the client is snapshotted off a live, unversioned engine surface (current-state-map risk #1, #10). Every edge here is a cross-service seam, each independently reversible (base-URL back, client re-pinned, ask re-aimed at the facade).
