## 🎯 Feature area — Verification spine & shared lib/auth verifier

Identity is on **every request's auth hot path** for the whole family. This epic builds the irreducible core: a multi-issuer resolver that verifies Clerk *and* Keycloak sessions behind one **IdP-agnostic principal** `{idp, subject, tenant, org_or_realm, roles, token_scope, exp}` (A-IDP), the `orvex-studio-lib/auth` verifier every satellite embeds (local JWKS, no per-request hop — A-VERIFY), the `POST /v1/introspect` liveness/scope/revocation path with a short Redis-cached TTL, and the **fail-closed grace with a hard wall-clock bound** that stops an attacker DoS-ing introspection to keep a revoked token alive (FR-I3). It exists because "satellites verify a caller by asking identity, never Clerk/Keycloak directly" — and the weakest satellite must not become the security ceiling.

Why it exists: PRD §4 F1 + Goals G1/G4; Architecture A-IDP / A-VERIFY. This is the FIRST-shipped surface — every other satellite's public surface depends on it (PRD §7 rollout; identity sequenced ahead of MCP).
