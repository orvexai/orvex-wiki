## 🧭 Epic — Janitors: consolidation, tiered compaction, hard delete (async only)

What keeps the store lean enough to keep sessions cheap — the field's weakest area, our
differentiator. This epic delivers the async janitors that run entirely off the hot path:
consolidation (Mem0-style ADD/UPDATE/DELETE/NOOP over top-k similar, preceded by a cheap
non-LLM 0.98-cosine intra-batch dedup, contradictions soft-deleted with a trail), tiered
compaction (tier-1 LLM summarization of closed items past window, tier-2 deep archive — both
v1, reference-aware, restorable from history), and hard cascading delete (right-to-erasure that
purges rows, history, summaries, embeddings and consolidation traces, leaving only a content-free
tombstone). The janitor step-API contract and the workflows schedules that trigger it round out
the epic, under a contractual no-train / EU-resident / zero-retention `orvex-studio-ai` provider config.

Why it exists: SM-5 (store leanness) and the counter-metrics SM-C1/C2 (volume is not success).
AD-5 pins the hard scope invariant: janitors operate ONLY on Remembered Facts and *closed* items
— open/in-progress/claimed items and pending handoffs are untouchable by the LLM loop (protects
SM-2). AD-5 also fixes erasure-wins precedence (FR-MEM13 overrides FR-MEM12's restore promise and
the reference-aware guard). NFR-MEM7 is conditional on the named provider contract.
