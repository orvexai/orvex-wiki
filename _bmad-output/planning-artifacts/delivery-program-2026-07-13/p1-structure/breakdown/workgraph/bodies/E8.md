## 🧭 Epic — Events, projection & console: the read-side fleet view

The read side of the blackboard. Every workgraph mutation emits a `studio.workgraph.<resource>.<past-tense>`
CloudEvent from the transactional outbox; knowledge indexes those rich events into Turbopuffer
(namespace-per-tenant) and projects them for the console. This epic delivers the event schemas
and the P9 source-adapter (`sources/workgraph.yaml`) with the fail-closed `acl_primitive`
(`{principal, candidate_ids[]} → allowed_ids[]`), the purge-event shape, the anomaly-detector
input signals (close `resolution` enum incl. `rejected`, a per-item re-open counter, edge-target
status), and OTel wiring via `pkg/obs` with the PII deny-list (item titles/notes/fact text never
in telemetry). It then delivers the console fleet view rendered from knowledge projections —
in-progress with lease freshness, ready, blocked-with-blockers, closed trail, the exactly-three
v1 anomaly detectors (stale claim, re-open loop, orphaned blocker), and pin/unpin of tenant facts.

Why it exists: UJ-3 — the fleet dashboard answers "what's happening?" without reading transcripts.
AD-7 makes `studio.workgraph.*` a new additive subdomain, forbids the console reading workgraph's
Postgres directly, and freezes the three-detector anomaly set. AD-3 binds the source-adapter's
one indexing path (rich-event-inline authoritative). The console view is dead until knowledge is
past its 501 scaffold — a known dependency gate.

- [ ] No item/note/fact content appears in telemetry (PII deny-list test). Family-E2E share green on dev (console read view).
