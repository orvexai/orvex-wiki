## 🧭 Epic — Claims, leases & handoff: atomic coordination without double-claims

The coordination integrity layer. When two agents race for the same ready item, exactly one
wins; when an agent crashes mid-work, its item comes back to ready without human intervention;
and when a planner hands work to a builder on a different platform, the transfer is first-class
and typed. This epic delivers the atomic `claim` CAS (assignee + status advanced in one
statement — no double-claim between concurrent agents), TTL leases with owner-only `heartbeat`
renewal, the `cmd/reaper` tick that reverts stale leases via a conditional CAS (staleness
predicate lives in the mutating statement — never read-then-revert-by-id), and the handoff
state machine (`submitted → claimed → accepted/returned`) derived from `(status, assignee,
resolution)` with targets resolved against identity-registered agent roles.

Why it exists: UJ-2's climax and its crash edge case. SM-2 is the metric — < 1% double-claims,
≥ 95% expired-lease recovery within the reaper grace window. AD-2 mandates claim as one CAS;
AD-5 pins the reaper's conditional-CAS revert and the `reaper_grace > heartbeat_interval`
invariant; AD-8 forbids handoff state living in a second free column.
