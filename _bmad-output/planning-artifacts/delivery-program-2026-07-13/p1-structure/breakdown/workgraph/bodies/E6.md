## 🧭 Epic — Authz & quota: namespaces outside, grants inside, 402 verdicts, degradation

One authorization chokepoint for the whole service, and one domain owner for cap verdicts.
This epic delivers the `authz` domain package that every read and mutation passes through —
MCP, CLI, interop adapters, step-APIs, and `ranking` all — enforcing namespace-prefix isolation
(`tenant / agent / session`) as the outer boundary no grant can cross, the 3-state privacy enum
(`open | private | shared-private`) inherited as design from the user-memory product, and
per-item agent/role-scoped read/write grants that may only *widen* the privacy floor. It adds
org-shared namespaces (polymorphic `{user|org}`, intra-org only), the `quota` domain package that
returns the frozen 402 `QUOTA_EXCEEDED` shape against billing-owned entitlements, and the
degradation posture (honest-empty prime/recall with status; claim stays CAS-safe under outage).

Why it exists: AD-9 forbids per-surface grant logic drifting and pins the precedence rule
(privacy enum = default floor, grants widen only, namespace caps both) plus the first-class
`system` principal for janitor/reaper/relay. AD-13 keeps cap logic out of handlers and freezes
the 402 shape. NFR-MEM4/6 bind isolation and degradation. The as-built trusted-header seam must
never recur.
