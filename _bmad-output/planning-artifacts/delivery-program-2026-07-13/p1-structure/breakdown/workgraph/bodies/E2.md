## 🧭 Epic — Work-graph core: items, edges, ready/blocked, batch, coordination primitives

The beads-derived substrate: the work items, typed edges, and ready-work computation that the
whole coordination surface reads and writes. Direct writes, no gate — latency is the product
(AD-2). This epic delivers item CRUD with append-only notes, the four v1 edge types with
`blocks` cycle rejection, the `ready`/`blocked`/`stats` queries backed by a single denormalized
`blocked` flag with incremental subgraph recompute, defer/undefer as an orthogonal dimension,
the single-transaction `batch` verb (the write-amplification killer for agent loops), the
data-plane coordination primitives (gate items, work templates, epic dispatch views), and the
pre-GA load test that publishes a measured degradation curve for the tenant envelope.

Why it exists: UJ-2 and UJ-3 live here — two agents handing off with zero context loss, and the
fleet dashboard's underlying state. AD-4 pins the model: `status ∈ {open, in-progress, closed}`
with `blocked`/`deferred`/`claimed` as orthogonal dimensions, `ready ≝ status=open ∧ ¬blocked ∧
¬deferred ∧ ¬claimed`, and ONE `recomputeBlocked(affectedSet)` primitive as the sole writer of
the flag. AD-6 gives every item a short id; scale is a published contract, never an asserted-flat claim.
