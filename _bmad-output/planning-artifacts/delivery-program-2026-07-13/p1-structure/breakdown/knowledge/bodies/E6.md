## 📖 Epic — Bulk export & bundles

The always-fresh tar/zip bundle builder relocates from the engine (FR-19) with the FR-35 audit spine imported intact (FR-K18/K19). Bundles are **single-request point-in-time ACL snapshots** built from the projection, streamed with a pre-stream non-erasable egress audit, live fail-closed export-policy evaluation, per-principal throttles, and blast-radius caps. Crucially, `userId` is derived from the **verified principal only** (never a request parameter — no confused deputy), and the artifact is a **principal-bound capability** (A-bundle): `GET /v1/bundles/:id` enforces `principal == creator AND tenant match`, closing the IDOR class.

Why it exists: bundles feed AI agents (tar bundles) and user full-export, and they transit the FR-22 suspended-tenant export guarantee (NFR-K5). Because a bundle is NOT re-filtered on fetch (it is a point-in-time snapshot), owner-binding is the sole safeguard — delivery is never a bare presigned S3 URL. Bundle builds draw ACL load through the A1a bulk bulkhead, never the interactive budget.
