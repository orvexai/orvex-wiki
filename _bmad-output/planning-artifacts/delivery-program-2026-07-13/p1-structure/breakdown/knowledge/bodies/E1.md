## 📖 Epic — Event ingestion & indexing pipeline

knowledge is the **first subscriber of the platform event spine** (PRD §1): every wiki mutation arrives as a typed `wiki.*` CloudEvent on the Kafka-first `studio-spine` broker, fed by the engine's transactional outbox + relay (Decision 6, D-S13). This epic builds the ingestion leg — the Knative Trigger subscription, the thin-event → export-pull → version-guarded CAS upsert path, the FR-K4 synchronous read-your-writes hook, chunking + embeddings, attachment (Tika) extraction, permission re-scope, and the failure/DLQ/staleness machinery. It is the freshness engine behind G2 ("a saved page is findable per the freshness contract") and closes the historical RAG-staleness bug class (FR-K28).

Why it exists: without event-fed freshness there is no retrieval backbone — polling was explicitly rejected (G2). The two-store write order (A2a: win Postgres CAS first, then Turbopuffer) and idempotent version guards (FR-K3) are what make redelivery/reordering safe, and the FR-K4 circuit breaker is what guarantees an engine save **never** fails on knowledge being down (NFR-K5).
