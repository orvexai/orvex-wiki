## 🗺️ Feature area

The engine is the **first and sole studio-spine producer** — every downstream consumer (knowledge indexing, billing, quota fan-out) depends on the engine getting the envelope and topic rules right. This epic replaces today's lossy best-effort `EventEmitter2 → Redis XADD` dual-write with the **transactional outbox**: one outbox row committed in the *same DB transaction* as each domain mutation (FR-W5 — covering the collab `onStoreDocument` write path), drained by a **worker-role relay** publishing **straight to Kafka as CloudEvents** (`partitionkey = workspaceId`) — the outbox + relay **IS** the mechanism, **no Redis→Kafka bridge is ever built** (D-S13, A-EVENTS). The engine ships its **own small TypeScript envelope builder** under `orvex/*` (it must not import the Apache-2.0 contracts helper across the AGPL boundary) that conforms to the frozen wire-names, proven against **golden fixtures** (A-SEAMS). Cell-contract producer duties land here: the `orvexcell` extension attribute (rule #6), `{domain}-events.{cell}` single-partition topics asserted at boot (rule #5), correlation-id threading and the relay liveness/lag heartbeat (A-OBSERVE finding F5 — a dead relay silently stops all emission while api `/api/health` stays green).

Why it exists: A-EVENTS + A-CELL — the event spine is the integration substrate the whole family rides; producer-before-consumer means satellite Triggers stay scale-to-zero until this relay produces.
