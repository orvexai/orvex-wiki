## 🧭 Feature Area

`pkg/auth` is the **family's security ceiling**: the single implementation of identity's dual-IdP, multi-issuer verifier and deny-by-default token-scope enforcement, embedded byte-identical in every Go satellite *and* in identity itself, so no satellite is the weakest link (PRD F1 / FR-L1–L8; Arch A-CEILING, A-VERIFY-IMPL, A-DENYLIST). Because identity's rollout names it "FIRST — every other satellite's public surface waits on it" (PRD §7), slippage here serializes the whole program. This epic also folds in the **cell apparatus** that is day-1-and-not-retrofittable: `cell`/`cell_epoch` claim parse (Rule 2) and the cell-guard 421 middleware (Rule 3), per the cell contract `[[JGAUQRsw2g]]` §5 and Arch A-CELL. The verifier's full behaviour is proved by the shipped `pkg/auth/authtest` conformance suite (V1–V12), which runs three ways as a release gate.

Why it exists: identity FR-I6 mandates scope enforcement live *here*, "never left to each satellite to reimplement." A scope-exceeding or revoked-token request must be rejected identically everywhere; insecurity must require deliberate code, not omitted config (NFR-L2).
