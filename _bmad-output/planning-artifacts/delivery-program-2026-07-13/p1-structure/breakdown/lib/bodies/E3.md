## 🧭 Feature Area

The four **typed, fail-closed clients** every Go satellite imports instead of hand-rolling a peer call: `pkg/engineclient` (block chokepoint, FR-13 ACL narrow, FR-18/38 export, export-policy, quota-status, provision/deprovision), `pkg/knowledgeclient` (query/retrieve/delta/rebuild step-API), `pkg/billingclient` (entitlements read — plans/quotas SoR) and `pkg/identityclient` (introspect/mint/lifecycle-mirror) — PRD F3/F4/F4b/F4c, FR-L12–L17, FR-L29, FR-L30; Arch A-CLIENTS. Every client is **fail-closed by construction**: no failure, empty response, or timeout is ever representable as "allow all" / "entitled" / "skip". The engine's F-QUOTA 402 is typed terminal and non-retryable (FR-L12a); the engine-vs-identity provision/deprovision vocabulary trap is two unrelated Go types with no conversion (A-CLIENTS; OQ-L5 closed → identityclient lands in lib).

Why it exists: "integrate at the end" collides with never-run seams (map risk #4). These clients make the safe path the only representable path — callers are structurally forced into knowledge's FR-K12 invariant (engine-down ⇒ fail closed).
