## 🎯 Story

As a **daemon on a multi-cell platform**, I want every cursor qualified by (cell/events-host, space) with an events-host cold-start on tenant-move, a ~6h belt-and-braces full reconcile, and offline `--cached` reads, so that cross-cell cursor aliasing never happens and freshness recovers even when the stream is unhealthy.

**Definition of Done:** ONE named test `TestCursorCellQualificationAndReconcile` — a daemon-layer test asserting, through the cursor store, that `sync_cursor_<cell/events-host>_<space>` keys per cell, that an events-host change cold-starts from `/events/head` (never resumes a foreign-cell cursor), that a ~6h reconcile runs regardless of stream health, and that reads succeed offline via `--cached`. *Final H1–H17 elaboration + exact contract tag/versions are pinned at pack certification (ENG-2105); this story is dispatch-blocked until that tag exists.*

## ✅ Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] **AC1** — Given cursors, When stored, Then they are keyed by (cell/events-host, space), not by space alone. *Assertion: `sync_cursor_<cell/events-host>_<space>` key form; no cross-cell aliasing.* [Source: finding F-C/NFR-CLI5]
- [ ] **AC2** — Given a tenant-move (events-host change), When detected, Then the daemon cold-starts from `/events/head` rather than resuming a cursor from another cell. *Assertion: cold-start on events-host change; foreign cursor discarded.* [Source: finding F-C]
- [ ] **AC3** — Given stream health (good or bad), When ~6h elapses, Then a belt-and-braces full reconcile runs. *Assertion: reconcile fires on the ~6h timer regardless of stream state.* [Source: FR-CLI16/NFR-CLI3]
- [ ] **AC4** — Given upstreams unreachable, When reading with `--cached`, Then reads succeed from the local cache with a typed staleness indication. *Assertion: `--cached` reads succeed offline; staleness typed.* [Source: NFR-CLI3]
- [ ] **AC5 (coordination)** — Given the E1-S5 421 re-resolve, When it re-points the events-host, Then the affected cursor is cold-started. *Assertion: 421 re-resolve triggers cursor cold-start (shared hook).* [Source: finding F-B/F-C]
