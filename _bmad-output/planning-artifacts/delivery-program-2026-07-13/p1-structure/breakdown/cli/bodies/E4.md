## 🎯 Epic — Offline DfM↔ProseMirror Conversion via pkg/dfm

The keystone. DfM↔ProseMirror conversion runs **in-process and offline** by embedding the clean-room `orvex-studio-lib/pkg/dfm` (D-A11) — the CLI is a pkg/dfm **embedder** and MUST NOT call any engine or wiki-api `/convert` endpoint. This closes the reference's hard offline gap (defect D1: `markdown→PM` hard-fails with no local path) and eliminates the baked-Excalidraw data-loss bug (defect D2) by construction via byte-lossless opaque round-trips reattached from the `ifVersion` base. The pkg/dfm swap is **gated on an AGPL-provenance audit** of the reference's hand-mirrored serializer and written clean-room from the documented PM schema + the contracts block-schema catalog + golden fixtures — never by reading AGPL TS (NFR-CLI7). The **golden-corpus parity test (FR-CLI21)** is the acceptance gate, carrying the ENG-1327/1328/897/898/1261 defences. Because round-trips are now lossless, the reference's compensating machinery (`.blocks.json` sidecars, embed-degradation refusals, unbounded conversion caches) collapses to a thin post-write receipt check. [Source: PRD R4AOVBLST7 §F5 FR-CLI12/13 + FR-CLI21; Architecture pf10XC2Qjz §Components pkg/dfm embedding]

Why it exists: offline authoring is the flagship CLI capability and the reason a shared Go serializer exists; clean provenance (G5/NFR-CLI7) is a legal precondition, and parity against the block-schema catalog is the only thing that lets the lossy-era machinery be dropped rather than ported.
