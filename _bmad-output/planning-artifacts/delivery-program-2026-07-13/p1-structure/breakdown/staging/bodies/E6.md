## 🎯 Feature area

"The Librarian constantly learns based on feedback" (Daniel). Every human accept/reject/edit is a labeled Feedback Event that tunes future recommendations. This epic builds the `learning` domain package: feedback capture linked to the exact prompt-pack version that produced the Disposition (FR-STG15); learning application via feedback-drawn few-shot exemplars, Trust-Tier recalibration, and Librarian-*proposed* pack revisions validated by a held-out exemplars-on/off harness, with admin-visible transparency (FR-STG16, FR-STG17); conservative cold-start defaults that embed no other tenant's content (FR-STG27); and a v1-minimal prompt-pack marketplace where installs land as admin-approval-gated proposed revisions (FR-STG29).

Why it exists: UJ-3 — the Librarian earns autonomy by learning; SM-2 requires ≥90% acceptance after 4 weeks. Learning must be attributable (every injection logged) and never self-applied (a proposed revision is itself a recommendation). Cold-start must be content-free by construction so no cross-tenant contamination is ever possible. [Source: mOzNDhn322 §4.3/§4.6 FR-STG15-17/27/29; GOacy9kdJz AD-14]
