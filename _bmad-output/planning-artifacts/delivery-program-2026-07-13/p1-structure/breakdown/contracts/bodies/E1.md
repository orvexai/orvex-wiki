**Feature area.** The v0.1 keystone deliverables that make orvex-studio-contracts a real, consumable, license-clean seam repo rather than a scaffold. Per SE-Arch T5, the repo's own *"only executable surface"* (self-validation CI), its declared *"health metric"* (`SEAMS.md`), the authored-not-generated provenance guard, and the versioning discipline (`VERSION`/`CHANGELOG`/first immutable tag) are all named-but-absent — this epic ships them as the foundation every other epic's artifact lands on. Nothing here runs in production; the repo is *"the agreement, not a participant"* (Architecture §1, PRD non-goal 1).

**Why it exists.** *"Build in isolation against the contract is impossible when the contract doesn't exist yet"* (current-state-map risk #1). Six+ repos pin this one repo; without a self-validation CI a malformed schema breaks all of them, and without immutable tags consumers float on `@dev` (violating the repo's own tag-pin rule — T5). This epic is the prerequisite that unblocks the whole family's isolated-build doctrine (brief Delivery Doctrine §1-2).
