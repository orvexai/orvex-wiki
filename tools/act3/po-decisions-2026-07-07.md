## OpenBao mint-500 ROOT CAUSE (heal agent, 2026-07-09) — supersedes the "transient flake" reading above

- Measured: leader (openbao-0) direct = **12/12 OK**; each standby (openbao-1, openbao-2) direct = **0/12**; round-robin `openbao` svc (what the public `secrets.*` endpoint hits) = ~½–⅔ OK; **leader-only `openbao-active` svc = 20/20 OK.** Same class also hits `harbor/token`.
- So the "~50% fleet-wide flake" = kube-proxy round-robin landing on 1 good leader vs 2 always-failing standbys. Independent of openbao-1's 20 restarts (openbao-2 fails identically). A mint-retry only "works" when a retry happens to hit the leader — masks the bug, leaves ⅔ of first-attempts red.
- NO cluster mutation made (a standby restart yields another read-only standby; deleting a pod needlessly drops failure-tolerance to 0).
