## OpenBao mint-500 ROOT CAUSE (heal agent, 2026-07-09) — supersedes the "transient flake" reading above
Full report: `scratchpad/openbao-heal.md`. Cluster is HEALTHY (3/3, AWS-KMS auto-unseal, Raft quorum intact, failure-tolerance 1, PVCs 0% full). The 500s are **deterministic, not flaky**:
- The mint reads `github/token/clone-only` (plugin `vault-plugin-secrets-github`). The token itself mints fine, but registering its **lease** is a Raft **storage write**, and a Raft **follower's storage is read-only** → `failed to register lease: cannot write to readonly storage` → 500. This read-op is served locally on the standby instead of being forwarded to the leader.
- Measured: leader (openbao-0) direct = **12/12 OK**; each standby (openbao-1, openbao-2) direct = **0/12**; round-robin `openbao` svc (what the public `secrets.*` endpoint hits) = ~½–⅔ OK; **leader-only `openbao-active` svc = 20/20 OK.** Same class also hits `harbor/token`.
- So the "~50% fleet-wide flake" = kube-proxy round-robin landing on 1 good leader vs 2 always-failing standbys. Independent of openbao-1's 20 restarts (openbao-2 fails identically). A mint-retry only "works" when a retry happens to hit the leader — masks the bug, leaves ⅔ of first-attempts red.
- NO cluster mutation made (a standby restart yields another read-only standby; deleting a pod needlessly drops failure-tolerance to 0).

### PO ESCALATION — pick one (Fix 1 recommended)
- **Fix 1 (safe, immediate, zero cluster risk):** route the mint at the leader-only path. `openbao-active` Service selects only the current leader and follows failover (the config HCL documents this; the snapshot CronJob already uses `openbao-active.openbao.svc:8200`).
